About

Want to learn more about the treatment or the app?

DeLaze is an app for Android that treats Amblyopia, or 'Lazy Eye', using experimental methods described in these research papers by R. Hess et al. Currently, it is one of the only ways to treat lazy eye in adults and adolescents, and it is far more effective than eye patching when treating children. Previous implementations have used more expensive versions of virtual reality headsets, but DeLaze uses the low cost Google Cardboard headset and nearly identical software to provide the high quality treatment at much lower prices.

DeLaze was made in 36 hours by Tristan Mortimer and Raphael Rouvinov at MHacks IV, where it was awarded 'Most Innovative Hack' from KPCB. For now it is exclusively for Android and features a Tetris-like game, but we intend to support iOS and add more games in the future. DeLaze is open source, in active development, and will always be free to use.

FAQ

If you're having problems or want to know more, check out through these first

Q: What is Google Cardboard? How can I get it?

A: Google Cardboard is a DIY virtual reality(VR) headset that utilizes Android smartphones to create a low cost VR experience. To get google Cardboard you can either order the parts separately and make it yourself, or you can get a pre-assembled headset or kit for under $10.

Q: I'm completely blind in on eye/one of my eyes is more closed than the other/my eye surgery failed, can this app help me?

A: Delaze is only effective in treating amblyopia, also known as 'Lazy Eye', and if you are unsure if you have amblyopia, then you should see your doctor before using this app. Delaze is not a miracle cure for every eye problem, and we don't want you to waste your time on the app if you don't need to.

Q: How often and how long should I use your app?

A: You will need to use Delaze for 15 minutes to an hour everyday in order for the treatment to be effective. For the quickest results, and especially for adults, the time spent on the app should be closer to the upper-end of that range.

Q: Is my phone compatible with Google Cardboard? Can it run your app?

A: Right now delaze only supports phones that run Android 4.1+. You can see a list of phones that are compatible with the Cardboard headset on the official website. If your phone is listed as incompatible with Cardboard, there's a fair chance our app does work on your phone but can't use the magnetic inputs. If that is the case, then you can buy a Cardboard headset that uses the touchscreen instead of the magnetometer from DODOcase.

Q: Hasn't lazyeyetetris.wordpress.com been around for a while?

A: Lazyeyetris.wordpress.com is a scam. There have been no studies that have shown 3D Anaglyph can be used to treat amblyopia this way, and the scientists I have talked to have also said that the website essentially pushes snake-oil. This scammer charges about $5 per app and none of them do anything for lazy eye, so don't waste your money.

Q: I'm having trouble moving the blocks left and right, what do I do?

A: try closing the app and starting it again after it is inside the headset and up to your face. DeLaze is still in beta and our motion inputs aren't perfect, so the app may be a little buggy. Fixes will come soon.

Q: Will DeLaze ever work on my iPhone?

A: Probably, but not just yet. Much of our app can easily be ported to iOS, and given that there are cardboard headsets that are compatible with iphone, we can say with confidence that it's not too far away.

Q: Are there going to be more games?

A: Maybe. Any 2D game that would force the use of binocular vision could be a treatment in theory, but we aren't 100% sure and would prefer to ask scientists about it before we add any more games.

Q: Is there any way I can contribute?

A: Yes! If you know someone who would benefit from this treatment, then tell them about it because they aren't going to hear it from their doctor. If you think you can contribute code, then please stop by our GitHub, we need any help we can get!
